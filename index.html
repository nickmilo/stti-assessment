This section articulates the differences between top-down "Architecting" or bottom-up "Gardening". Neither style is better than the other. Both are needed.

Convergent Thinker

Healthy Architecting ‚ñº

You enjoy seeing the big picture. When possible, you prefer knowing more about a subject or situation before you act. You like categorizing, and you use the big picture to further your sensemaking and decision-making. You are a good organizer and benefit from having a few lists and maps to guide your actions. The question is, can your convergent thinking be loose enough to allow for emergent thinking?

Fragile Thinker

Unhealthy Architecting ‚ñº

When taken too far, your tendency to architect can lead to stifling, rigid thinking. When that need sets in, you can become paralyzed when you don't have enough structure. This can manifest in coming across as needy and overburdening others. Sometimes it can even turn brainstorming sessions into prosecutions.

Divergent Thinker

Healthy Gardening ‚ñº

You are comfortable without having a complete high-level picture. Instead, you are able to work with limited information and follow the trail of enthusiasm, clues, and connections. You generate a lot of thoughts. The question is, can your divergent thinking lead to emergent thinking?

Scattered Thinker

Unhealthy Gardening ‚ñº

When taken too far, your tendency to garden can lead to a chronic sense of anxiety around trying to find things‚Äîwhether it's car keys or old notes. One tendency of unhealthy bottom-up thinking is to chronically collect new and exciting information...never to touch it again.

Understanding Your Sensemaking Archetypes
S

Synthesizer

Synthesizers want to collect, reorganize and reframe knowledge. Whether the source information is the dozens of sparks flying through their mind in a day or multiple books, articles and videos, synthesizers are driven to make connections among disparate ideas and find clarity in complexity. This archetype is embodied by professional researchers, educators, students, and deep thinkers in all disciplines.

P

Producer

Producers want to flourish in their careers and get things done. They are driven to apply what they've learned to clear outcomes, embracing workflows and systems that help them to be more efficient and effective in tracking progress and moving efforts forward, taking action and prioritizing tangible results. This archetype is embodied by entrepreneurs, managers, students at particular points in their learning, and anyone who is aspiring for professional or academic growth.

I

Inner Guide

Inner Guides want to nourish their minds and reflect on their place in the world. Self-exploration and self-discovery are most important to them, with the end goal being the cultivation of a meaningful life where values, intentions and actions are aligned. This archetype is embodied by lifelong learners, personal development enthusiasts, people at transition points in life searching for clarity on who they are and where they want to go, and those who feel a strong need to move away from the noise of other people's thoughts and find grounding and calmness in their own.

C

Creative

Creatives want to express their inner selves in unique and meaningful ways. They are driven to develop skills in the medium of their choice so they can share their knowledge and experiences in ways that are helpful and impactful to others. It's not always obvious why a creative is spending time on certain things, but those things often flavor their thoughts and ideas in unique ways. This archetype is embodied by active or aspiring authors, writers, and creators of all kinds.
